Welcome to the Firestarter Digital Portal

Each month, you will find new original content published through our digital portal. Here, we give insight into our ongoing work, share latest research publications, and give unique access to our ongoing media production.

This month, we are excited to share, We Are In It, a film we produced in 2016 about the experiences of migrant communities living across Houston, TX.



Firestarter film production works exclusively with people we love. Our films are directed by Yehuda Sharim and available through a variety of distribution mediums. Information about upcoming screenings can be found at the links below, along with special features and full press kit content.

We Are In It (2016)

We are in it features visceral scenes from the everyday lives of Karla, Serges, Hussein, Nancy and Tutu. For all of them, Houston is their common space of struggle, pleasure, and shelter. For them, Houston is a second, third or even fourth city of residence, both home and metropolis of hostility. Here, they are safe, restless, part of a diaspora that struggles to find meaning beyond labels of foreigner, immigrant, undocumented, alien, and refugee.

Lessons in Seeing (2017)

When the elderly math teacher, Sibhatleab, ventures alone from a refugee camp in Ethiopia, he finds himself at the bottom rung of the social ladder in a vast, faceless Texan metropolis in the summer before a contentious election. As he still copes with his unthinkable twenty-five year history of imprisonment and torture, he finds hope at Salem Market, a tiny store that caters to Houston's refugee community. The film tells his story of disillusionment and resilience, questioning our understanding of the immigrant experience in contemporary America.

Seeds of All Things (2018)

Amid the backdrop of a contentious presidential election, a health clinic in Southwest Houston is run by and for immigrants and refugees. A family from Iran is bound by love as they build a new home in the city's most diverse neighborhood.
